COURSE DETAILS: INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY

WHAT DOES AN IT CAREER ENTAIL?

Computers, storage, and networking tools are used in information technology (IT) to produce, process, store, distribute, and safeguard electronic data. It is a rapidly expanding industry, partly as a result of the increased demand for IT services across all industries. IT degree programmes prepare students to work for IT departments and consulting organisations and earn potentially significant incomes. According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for IT jobs was $81,430 in May 2015, which is much higher than the average for all occupations. Learn more about how to get started in the IT industry, including the many career options and educational requirements for employment, by reading on.

The university awarding the certificate may provide a wide range of information technology degree programmes. There are educational alternatives to fit practically any budget or schedule, ranging from conventional schools and universities to accredited online and distant learning degree programmes. In specific fields of information technology, some colleges offer certificate programmes. Others provide associate, bachelor's, or master's degree programmes that are intended to develop well-rounded individuals with a comprehensive knowledge foundation in a variety of information technology fields.

Both on-campus and online options are available if you're seeking for information technology degree programmes. If you want to enrol in a distant school without moving from where you live, an online programme can be a wonderful option. Online degrees in information technology are actually available in a wide variety of formats. These include totally online programmes, accelerated online options, joint degree programmes, and hybrid programmes, which combine campus-based and distance learning courses. The majority of online computer technology degree programmes also make use of a learning management system that lets you submit assignments, ask questions, and engage in discussions with other students.

COURSE DETAILS: BUSINESS ADMINISTRATION

WHAT DOES AN IT CAREER ENTAIL?

Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA) degree is intended to provide in-depth knowledge of a variety of business operations-related topics. The program's core curriculum often includes lessons in accounting, marketing, finance, and other subjects. At a typical college, it may take up to four years or longer to complete the degree; however, in an accelerated programme, it can be completed considerably more quickly. You might have developed critical thinking abilities and practical expertise in one or more speciality areas as a graduate. You will have the chance to study from instructors with a wealth of business administration knowledge thanks to the program's goal. You get the opportunity to meet with like-minded people from diverse walks of life through the course as well.

Career Objectives

Enrolling in the BBA program you can learn more about business theory and its real-world applications. In the end, you could gain an understanding of various aspects of business administration, including:

  • Developing strategic plans using marketing information
  • Understand the role of information technology systems (IT) in supporting business operations
  • Learn how to solve organizational problems using human resources and management principles
  • Understand how to use data tools to gather business intelligence. Proceed to use the information to make key operational decisions
  • Learn to apply critical thinking concepts
  • Develop the ability to apply financial management principles to ensure viability

COURSE DETAILS: LAND SURVEY

WHAT DOES AN IT CAREER ENTAIL?

Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA) degree Students who enrol in the Geospatial Sciences (Building Information Modelling) MSc programme learn the theory, resources, and methods for dealing with geospatial data, from collection to processing to analysis. Through seminars and a dissertation, students have the chance to interact and collaborate with academics and industrial partners on cutting-edge research. Students will learn about the geometric and semantic components of BIM as well as the integrated management of geospatial and BIM-related data through the specialised Building Information Modelling (BIM) programme. A comprehensive review of technologies connected to mapping sciences, geo-information science, and 3D geometric modelling are included, as well as surveying principles and 3D reality capture.
Careers
With a degree in geospatial sciences (building information modelling), graduates will be prepared for a wide range of positions, such as BIM or surveying manager, geospatial data scientist, geospatial software developer, and consultant. Graduates find employment with a variety of businesses, including large infrastructure projects, engineering consultancies, geospatial software firms, and national mapping organisations. Through the industrial seminar series and industrial research projects, our students forge close connections with business. Additionally, a large number of alumni from our programmes go on to do PhD research at UCL or elsewhere.
Employability
Employers place a high value on a variety of technical skills that students will learn in geospatial science, such as using specialised software for surveying, Reality Capture, BIM and GIS (such as Cyclone, PhotoScan, Revit, QGIS, FME); programming, web and software development (such as Python, R, MATLAB); geospatial data processing and management; and geospatial data visualisation.

Career Objectives

The areas in which the knowledge and technology of Surveying are today applied include:

  • Precise determination of the figure of the earth and of other planets in the solar system
  • Measurement and mapping of topography
  • Measurement and determination of orbits of artificial satellites
  • Measurement and determination of the dynamics of the earths crust
  • Determination and analysis of the earths gravity field
  • Precise positioning on, or near, the earths surface using both terrestrial and satellite technology techniques
  • Navigation and guidance of craft using satellite and inertial navigation systems technology
  • Vehicle tracking and intelligent transport systems (ITS)
